Project Summary
Artificial Intelligence has emerged as a transformative force across various domains, and
its influence within education continues to expand. In modern academic environments,
the need for automated classroom monitoring has become increasingly essential as institu-
tions strive to enhance teaching quality, ensure transparency, and streamline administrative
responsibilities. The proposedAI-Based Classroom Monitoring and Video Summa-
rization Systemaddresses these needs by intelligently analyzing classroom videos and ex-
tracting meaningful insights without manual intervention. Through advanced deep learning
techniques, includingI3Dfor spatiotemporal feature extraction andLSTMfor sequential
activity recognition, the system identifies key instructional behaviors such as lecturing,
writing on the board, or interacting with students. Furthermore, the system generates con-
cise summaries and highlight segments that encapsulate the core moments of a classroom
session, thus enabling administrators to review lengthy lectures efficiently. In addition, the
punctuality detection moduleevaluates instructor arrival and departure times by com-
paring detected activity patterns with predefined timetables, producing automated alerts
for late arrivals, early departures, or absences. All processed data is stored in structured
form and displayed on a responsive web dashboard built withReactandTailwind CSS.
The dashboard provides real-time access to summaries, highlights, punctuality logs, and
system-generated notifications, ensuring that stakeholders remain informed and engaged.
As a whole, the project introduces a scalable, modular, and intelligent system that reduces
manual workload, improves institutional oversight, and contributes to effective academic
management. The architecture also offers flexibility for future enhancements such as real-
time monitoring, behavioral analytics, and integration with external academic systems,
making it a robust solution for modern educational environments.

1.1 Introduction
Artificial Intelligence (AI) has become a driving force behind the digital transformation of
various industries, enabling automation, intelligent decision-making, and performance op-
timization. Within the academic domain, AI has contributed to enhanced teaching method-
ologies, e-learning platforms, automated grading systems, performance analytics, and im-
proved administrative workflows. However, despite these advancements, classroom activity
monitoring remains heavily dependent on traditional manual practices.
In many educational institutions, supervisors are required to observe teachers manually,
record punctuality, verify lesson delivery, evaluate engagement, and ensure adherence to
schedules. These conventional methods are time-intensive, inconsistent, and difficult to
scale—especially as the number of teachers and classrooms continues to increase. Manual
monitoring also introduces subjectivity and is often unable to capture detailed activity pat-
terns throughout the entire class duration.
Recent developments in deep learning, particularly Inflated 3D Convolutional Networks
(I3D) and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) models, have enabled machines to analyze
and understand human activities within video sequences. I3D networks efficiently extract
spatial-temporal features from visual data, while LSTM models accurately interpret activity
progression across time. When combined, these technologies create a strong foundation for
automated classroom video analysis.
By applying these approaches to academic environments, classroom videos can be pro-
cessed to identify teacher actions, summarize key moments, evaluate punctuality, and gen-
erate intelligent alerts. This establishes a significant opportunity to build an AI-based
Classroom Monitoring System capable of improving transparency, enhancing institutional
accountability, and reducing administrative workload.
1.2 Background
Artificial Intelligence has gained prominence worldwide due to its ability to analyze com-
plex data, automate repetitive tasks, and offer insights that were traditionally difficult to

--- Page 20 ---
obtain. In education, AI-driven applications have modernized teaching, assessment, and
student support. Despite these advancements, monitoring teaching activities inside class-
rooms still follows conventional, human-dependent procedures.
Administrators often rely on manual inspections to confirm whether a teacher arrived on
time, completed the required activities, or followed the assigned schedule. These processes
are limited by human fatigue, subjectivity, delayed reporting, and a lack of continuous
observation. As institutions grow larger, maintaining consistent monitoring becomes in-
creasingly challenging.
With the emergence of powerful vision-based models like I3D and LSTM, it is now possi-
ble to automate the interpretation of classroom video data. These models can detect actions
such as explaining lessons, writing on the board, reading material, sitting, interacting with
students, or using mobile devices. Automated analysis can support administrators in gener-
ating summaries, activity logs, punctuality evaluations, and performance trends with much
higher accuracy and consistency compared to traditional methods.
This forms the foundation for designing a comprehensive Classroom Monitoring System
that leverages AI to enhance academic supervision and institutional governance.
1.3 Problem Statement
Although education is rapidly evolving toward digital transformation, institutions still lack
an intelligent and automated mechanism to analyze classroom activities using video data.
Current classroom monitoring procedures rely primarily on manual observation, making
the process inconsistent, time-consuming, and difficult to manage on a large scale. Several
challenges arise due to this dependency on human evaluation:
•Absence of automated teacher punctuality tracking:Institutions currently do not
possess a system capable of identifying and analyzing teacher arrival and departure
times through classroom videos.
•Unavailability of activity recognition tools:Existing platforms are unable to clas-
sify actions such as explaining content, writing on the board, reading, sitting, inter-
acting with students, or using mobile devices during class sessions.

•Lack of automatic highlight extraction:Reviewing long video recordings manu-
ally makes it extremely difficult to extract key classroom moments efficiently.
•Inability to evaluate schedule adherence:Without intelligent automation, it be-

and classroom responsibilities.
•Absence of automated alert mechanisms:Deviations such as late arrivals, early
departures, or irregular activity patterns remain undetected due to the lack of a smart
notification system.
•Unavailability of an integrated solution:Institutions do not yet have a unified

These limitations hinder effective quality assurance, reduce institutional transparency, and
create obstacles in performance monitoring. Developing an AI-based Classroom Moni-

evaluation, and improving overall operational efficiency.

Video Summarization Using Deep Neural Networks: A Survey
Evlampios Apostolidis, Eleni Adamantidou, Alexandros I. Metsai, Vasileios Mezaris, Ioan-
nis Patras
Proceedings of the IEEE, 109(11), Nov. 2021
Problem Statement
Video summarization aims to generate a concise synopsis by selecting the most informative
parts of the video content. This task involves identifying key frames or segments that best
represent the video’s main events or themes.
Methods Used
The paper presents a comprehensive survey of deep learning-based methods for video
summarization, categorizing them into supervised, unsupervised, and weakly-supervised
approaches. It discusses the use of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for feature
extraction, Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks for capturing temporal dependen-
cies, and Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) for unsupervised summarization.
Results / Key Findings
The survey highlights the evolution of deep learning techniques in video summarization,
emphasizing the importance of temporal dynamics and semantic understanding. It also dis-
cusses the challenges in evaluating summarization methods and the need for standardized
benchmarks.
Relation to Proposed Project
Your classroom activity recognition project shares similarities with video summarization
tasks, particularly in identifying and classifying significant segments (e.g., writing on board,
hand raising). The deep learning techniques discussed in this paper, such as CNNs for fea-

provides:
• Video upload interface
• Access to summaries and video highlights

The backend layer acts as the bridge between the frontend and the AI processing module.
Its responsibilities include:
• Receiving video uploads
• Managing API endpoints
• Sending video data to the AI model for processing
• Returning outputs such as summaries, highlights, alerts, and punctuality records
4.2.3 AI Processing Layer (I3D + LSTM Engine)
This layer is responsible for all AI-driven video analysis. Major operations include:
• Extracting frames from classroom videos
• Generating spatial-temporal features using I3D
• Predicting classroom actions through LSTM
• Producing video summaries and key highlights
• Detecting teacher arrival and departure times
• Calculating punctuality by comparing detected times with the timetable
• Generating alerts for late arrival, early departure, or absence

This layer manages the structured storage of:
• Teacher profiles
• Timetable information

The overall system workflow proceeds as follows:
1. The administrator uploads a classroom video through the dashboard.

4. All processed results are stored in the database and storage layer.
5. The frontend retrieves this data and presents it to the administrator through an inter-

5.1 Conclusion
This project demonstrates the need for an automated and intelligent solution to overcome
the limitations of traditional classroom monitoring. Manual evaluation is time-consuming,
inconsistent, and impractical for large educational institutions. By integrating advanced
deep learning models such as I3D and LSTM with computer vision techniques, the pro-
posed AI-Based Classroom Monitoring System offers an efficient and scalable alternative.
The system is designed to analyze recorded classroom videos, detect teacher activities,
generate summaries and key highlights, identify arrival and departure times, and compare
them with institutional timetables. Through these features, the project aims to enhance
transparency, support administrative decision-making, and reduce human effort in class-
room supervision. Although the system depends on predefined actions, camera quality,
and GPU resources, it still provides a highly innovative and valuable framework for educa-
tional monitoring.
Overall, this documentation outlines the foundation, objectives, scope, limitations, and con-
tributions of the system, establishing a strong basis for the technical design and implemen-
tation phases. The project ultimately contributes to modernizing educational management
by introducing AI-driven automation, reliability, and actionable insights.
